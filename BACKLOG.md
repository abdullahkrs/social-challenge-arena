## Items

### B-2026-001 — Signal Sprint

- Product value: a very short seeded visual-reaction challenge could broaden the future catalog with a reflex-focused session and simple same-sequence competition.
- Target audience: users who prefer rapid tap/swipe play and repeated attempts.
- 2D/3D opportunity: 2D is the strongest fit; 2.5D or 3D would add mostly decorative cost unless a later concept proves a real depth-based decision.
- Islamic-content considerations: use neutral abstract cues, no prohibited themes, no betting or chance rewards, friendly comparison language, and an approved silent-safe audio approach.
- Language considerations: gameplay can be language-light, but discovery, controls, results, challenge links, comparison, errors, and accessibility must support Arabic RTL, English, and Turkish.
- Dependencies: evidence that the shared challenge-link and comparison loop works; originality review; latency and input-fairness design; Islamic, visual, motion, localization, and QA gates.
- Reason deferred: the mechanic is crowded, reaction-time scores can overfavor device latency or younger users, and it offers less accessibility confidence than Trace Relay for the first slice.
- Revisit trigger: Trace Relay validates same-seed friend competition and Product Health identifies demand for a materially different reflex mechanic.

### B-2026-002 — Bridge Builder Dash

- Product value: a visually stronger placement/physics challenge could demonstrate 2.5D or lightweight 3D capability and produce share-worthy near-miss and result moments.
- Target audience: casual players and competitive friends who enjoy timing, placement, and distance improvement.
- 2D/3D opportunity: 2.5D is preferred for depth readability and spectacle; 2D remains possible for low-end fallback; 3D requires a concrete camera and interaction benefit plus an approved rendering decision.
- Islamic-content considerations: use neutral safe environments, no unsafe real-world imitation, no gambling or chance reward, no humiliating failure presentation, and policy-approved visual/audio treatment.
- Language considerations: gameplay geometry should remain language-independent; Arabic RTL, English, and Turkish must cover shell, HUD, result, comparison, errors, and accessibility.
- Dependencies: proven shared shell and lifecycle; mobile physics and rendering budget; low-end fallback; original visual direction; Islamic, motion, localization, and QA approval.
- Reason deferred: physics, camera, assets, loading, performance, and maintenance would obscure whether the basic social funnel works.
- Revisit trigger: one lightweight 2D vertical slice ships successfully and Product Health recommends expanding the catalog into a materially different 2.5D or 3D experience.
