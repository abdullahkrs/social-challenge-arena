# Product Brief

## Product vision

Build a continuously evolving multilingual social arcade platform for broad social-media audiences.

The platform must support both polished 2D and lightweight-to-rich 3D challenge games. New games, visual directions, social mechanics, languages, and improvements should be proposed, prioritized, and delivered continuously by coordinated specialized agents without requiring the repository owner to define every release.

## Core user promise

A user should be able to:

Discover a compelling challenge → understand it within seconds → play on mobile → receive a clear result → share one link or result → a friend competes → both compare → the interaction encourages replay or another share.

## Audience

Design for a broad cross-section of social-media users, including:

- casual players;
- competitive friends;
- families;
- younger and older users where the content is age-appropriate;
- Arabic-speaking, English-speaking, Turkish-speaking, and future language communities;
- users on low-end and high-end mobile devices;
- users who prefer reduced motion, keyboard access, or simplified interaction.

Do not optimize the product for one platform, age group, culture, device class, or game genre only.

## Product dimensions

The catalog may include:

- 2D arcade games;
- 2.5D games;
- lightweight 3D games;
- richer 3D games when performance and value justify them;
- reaction, timing, memory, rhythm, physics, aiming, racing, stacking, puzzle, survival, and other original mechanics;
- asynchronous social competition;
- seasonal and trend-informed challenges;
- creator-friendly or community-friendly formats when safely validated.

2D and 3D are both first-class product capabilities. Agents decide which dimension best serves each approved concept.

## Autonomous product planning

The repository owner sets the durable vision and constraints. The agent team decides:

- roadmap stages;
- release scope;
- game sequence;
- whether a concept should be 2D, 2.5D, or 3D;
- when shared infrastructure is justified;
- which languages are implemented next;
- which experiments, improvements, or retirements are needed;
- when to improve an existing game versus introduce a new challenge.

Agents must base decisions on product value, evidence, feasibility, originality, safety, accessibility, performance, and maintainability.

They must not wait for owner approval for ordinary roadmap sequencing once this brief and the operating system are active. Major changes to the product vision, Islamic constraints, privacy model, or commercial model still require owner approval.

## Islamic values and content policy

All product decisions, games, visuals, text, audio, monetization concepts, social mechanics, and trend adaptations must respect Islamic values.

The platform must not include or promote:

- nudity, sexualized characters, sexual content, or suggestive presentation;
- gambling, betting, loot-box gambling patterns, or chance-based monetary rewards;
- alcohol, drugs, smoking, or prohibited-substance promotion;
- occult, worship, polytheistic ritual, or religious mockery;
- humiliation, bullying, indecency, vulgarity, or abusive social pressure;
- music, sound, imagery, or themes that the approved Islamic content policy explicitly prohibits;
- deceptive monetization, exploitation of children, or manipulative dark patterns;
- unsafe physical challenges;
- content that attacks religions, peoples, cultures, families, or protected groups.

When a trend is culturally or religiously questionable, agents must reject it or extract only a safe generic mechanic without reproducing the problematic content.

Uncertain Islamic-content decisions must be marked blocked and escalated for owner review rather than guessed.

## Multilingual requirement

Multilingual support is mandatory from the product architecture onward.

Initial priority languages:

1. Arabic with complete RTL support.
2. English.
3. Turkish.

Agents may recommend additional languages based on audience opportunity and evidence.

Requirements:

- one shared localization system;
- language-independent game and share links;
- no duplicated application by language;
- full translation of discovery, gameplay, results, sharing, comparison, errors, accessibility, and legal or safety text;
- correct document direction, mixed-direction handling, number presentation, wrapping, and long-label behavior;
- original culturally appropriate wording rather than literal low-quality translation.

## Graphics and animation

Graphics, animation, and game feel are core product requirements.

Every approved game must define:

- visual direction;
- motion direction;
- player, world, obstacle, and result presentation;
- at least three purposeful feedback effects where appropriate;
- reduced-motion behavior;
- performance budgets for target devices;
- asset ownership and originality;
- a share-worthy visual moment.

2D games may use vector art, sprites, procedural graphics, canvas, or DOM rendering.

3D games may use an approved web-rendering approach only after a focused architecture decision confirms mobile performance, bundle size, accessibility, fallback behavior, and maintainability.

## Continuous evolution

The platform must maintain a continuous pipeline:

Public trend or product opportunity → original concept → product and Islamic-content review → visual and motion direction → vertical slice → QA → release → product-health review → improve, expand, retain, or retire.

No more than two consecutive merged tasks may produce no visible user improvement unless a verified critical foundation blocker requires it.

## Product decision principles

Agents should prefer:

- complete vertical slices over disconnected modules;
- clear fun within five seconds;
- short replayable sessions;
- strong social competition hooks;
- original visual identity;
- mobile performance;
- broad accessibility;
- reusable platform capabilities without building a generic engine prematurely;
- measurable improvements;
- a varied catalog rather than cosmetic reskins.

## Non-negotiable shared systems

Games should reuse shared platform systems where appropriate:

- discovery;
- lifecycle and teardown contracts;
- result handling;
- strict link validation;
- friend challenge flow;
- comparison;
- share-again;
- localization;
- accessibility;
- privacy-safe metrics;
- content and Islamic-compliance review;
- asset loading and performance checks.

## Success definition

The platform is succeeding when it repeatedly delivers original, polished, safe, multilingual 2D and 3D social challenges that users can understand quickly, enjoy, replay, share, and use to compete with friends—while the agent team continues to make coherent product decisions without owner micromanagement.
