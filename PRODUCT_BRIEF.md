# Product Brief

## Product vision

Build a continuously evolving multilingual social arcade platform for broad social-media audiences.

The platform must support both polished 2D and lightweight-to-rich 3D challenge games. New games, visual directions, social mechanics, languages, and improvements should be proposed, prioritized, and delivered continuously by coordinated specialized agents without requiring the repository owner to define releases or resolve ordinary decisions.

## Core user promise

A user should be able to:

Discover a compelling challenge → understand it within seconds → play on mobile → receive a clear result → share one link or result → a friend competes → both compare → the interaction encourages replay or another share.

## Audience

Design for a broad cross-section of social-media users, including casual players, competitive friends, families, younger and older users where age-appropriate, Arabic-speaking, English-speaking, Turkish-speaking, and future language communities, low-end and high-end mobile devices, reduced-motion users, keyboard users, and users who prefer simplified interaction.

Do not optimize the product for one platform, age group, culture, device class, or game genre only.

## Product dimensions

The catalog may include 2D arcade games, 2.5D games, lightweight and richer 3D games where justified, original reaction, timing, memory, rhythm, physics, aiming, racing, stacking, puzzle, survival, seasonal, trend-informed, creator-friendly, and community-friendly challenges when safely validated.

2D and 3D are both first-class product capabilities. Agents decide which dimension best serves each approved concept.

## Autonomous product planning

The agent team decides roadmap stages, release scope, game sequence, rendering dimension, shared infrastructure, language sequencing, experiments, improvements, retirements, and whether to improve an existing game or introduce a new challenge.

Agents must base decisions on product value, evidence, feasibility, originality, safety, accessibility, performance, maintainability, and reversibility. They must not wait for owner approval or refer choices back to the owner.

When a proposed capability falls outside the approved vision—such as payments, accounts, public feeds, public UGC, gambling, invasive data collection, or a materially different commercial model—the team must omit it, record it as out of scope, and continue with the highest-value compliant alternative.

## Islamic values and content policy

All product decisions, games, visuals, text, audio, monetization concepts, social mechanics, and trend adaptations must respect Islamic values.

The platform must not include or promote nudity, sexualized or suggestive presentation, gambling or betting, chance-based monetary rewards, prohibited substances, occult or worship simulation, religious mockery, humiliation, bullying, indecency, vulgarity, abusive social pressure, explicitly prohibited music or imagery, deceptive monetization, exploitation of children, manipulative dark patterns, unsafe physical challenges, or attacks on religions, peoples, cultures, families, or protected groups.

When a trend is culturally or religiously questionable, agents must reject it or extract only a safe generic mechanic without reproducing the problematic content.

When Islamic-content permissibility is unclear, agents must use the most conservative clearly compliant treatment, remove or replace the disputed element, and reject the concept if necessary. The decision must not be referred to the owner and must not block the pipeline.

## Multilingual requirement

Multilingual support is mandatory from the product architecture onward.

Initial priority languages:

1. Arabic with complete RTL support.
2. English.
3. Turkish.

Agents may add languages based on audience opportunity and evidence.

Requirements include one shared localization system, language-independent game and share links, no duplicated application by language, full translation of all required surfaces, correct document direction, mixed-direction handling, number presentation, wrapping, long-label behavior, accessible announcements, safe fallback behavior, and culturally appropriate original wording.

## Graphics and animation

Graphics, animation, and game feel are core product requirements.

Every approved game must define visual direction, motion direction, player/world/obstacle/result presentation, purposeful feedback effects, reduced-motion behavior, device performance budgets, asset ownership and originality, and a share-worthy visual moment.

2D games may use vector art, sprites, procedural graphics, canvas, or DOM rendering. 3D games may use an approved web-rendering approach only after a focused architecture decision confirms mobile performance, bundle size, accessibility, fallback behavior, and maintainability.

## Continuous evolution

The platform maintains this continuous pipeline:

Public trend or product opportunity → original concept → visual and motion direction → compliant vertical slice → localization → QA → release → product-health review → improve, expand, retain, retire, or select the next concept.

No more than two consecutive merged tasks may produce no visible user improvement unless a verified critical foundation blocker requires it.

## Product decision principles

Agents should prefer complete vertical slices, clear fun within five seconds, short replayable sessions, strong social competition hooks, original visual identity, mobile performance, broad accessibility, reusable platform capabilities without premature generic engines, measurable improvements, and a varied catalog rather than cosmetic reskins.

## Non-negotiable shared systems

Games should reuse discovery, lifecycle and teardown contracts, result handling, strict link validation, friend challenge flow, comparison, share-again, localization, accessibility, privacy-safe metrics, Islamic-policy checks, asset loading, and performance checks where appropriate.

## Success definition

The platform succeeds when it repeatedly delivers original, polished, safe, multilingual 2D and 3D social challenges that users can understand quickly, enjoy, replay, share, and use to compete with friends—while the agent team continues coherent autonomous product decisions without owner involvement.
