## Trace Relay selected creative contract

- Selected treatment: **Ribbon Relay**.
- Core fantasy: receive an abstract route handoff, remember it, reproduce it, and pass the same hidden challenge to a friend.
- Round one: five anchors, four route selections.
- Round two: seven anchors, five route selections.
- Round three: nine anchors, six route selections, with at most one non-consecutive anchor revisit.
- Wrong input does not erase completed progress or restart the run.
- Optional show-next assistance reveals only the next required anchor and removes first-try mastery for that position.
- Finish-now creates a friendly partial result.
- The share-worthy result transforms completed rounds into a generic three-band relay mark without field geometry, route segments, anchor order, seed, solution, or active-play screenshot.
- Rematch creates a fresh deterministic challenge; share-again is voluntary and never blocks continuation.

## Trace Relay visual contract

- Visual brief: `concepts/trace-relay/VISUAL_BRIEF.md`.
- Identity: **Folded Signal**—a broad route ribbon through an asymmetric field of rounded irregular folded-marker plates.
- Logical board: `1000 × 1250`, displayed at `4:5`, centered from 288 to 520 CSS px wide, and never mirrored in RTL.
- Anchor family: nine original silhouettes with separate internal signatures; no identical dots, letters, numerals, faces, sacred/occult signs, flags, cultural emblems, or brand marks.
- Field rules: deterministic asymmetric placement; reject straight rows/columns, ring-like arrangements, insufficient spacing, route crossings through unrelated targets, and keypad-like layouts.
- State clarity: reveal, hidden-ready, focus, pressed, correct, incorrect, assisted, disabled, and completed states use outline, pattern, line construction, accessible announcements, and optional color.
- Route treatment: solid reveal ribbon with readability edge; double-line player trace; deterministic crossing break; incorrect input draws no misleading segment; full concealment before input.
- HUD and shell: labels remain outside the board; Arabic RTL and English/Turkish LTR use logical layout; long labels wrap without covering anchors; no directional icon is required for a core action.
- Result: fixed three-band relay mark encodes mastery/corrected/assisted/incomplete state without encoding route, field, seed, identity, or locale.
- Comparison: equal player weight, neutral lead/tie marker, no podium, crown, dimmed loser, destructive wording, urgency, or public rank.
- Share: generic spoiler-safe square and landscape compositions; no active board, route, seed, sender personal data, fake metrics, or humiliating copy.
- Assets: repository-owned procedural/vector primitives; critical visual target 35 KB gzip and ceiling 50 KB; no critical raster, video, model, texture atlas, or game-specific font.
- Runtime visual target: no more than 9 anchors, target 180 and ceiling 240 active-board nodes, bounded optional fragments, 30 fps minimum and 60 fps target on capable devices.
- Low-end mode removes texture, depth, blur, decorative fragments, and idle motion while preserving geometry, state, focus, targets, score access, and actions.

## Trace Relay concept constraints

- Keep the typical first session around 30–45 seconds, understandable from a brief skippable demonstration, and playable without registration.
- Do not imitate an operating-system unlock grid, Simon-style trade dress, known memory toy, branded palette, copied sound, copied wording, character, level, or recognizable presentation.
- Never use a standard 3×3 dot grid, security/unlock metaphor, four colored pads, cumulative sound loop, hearts/lives, fail buzzers, skulls, flames, crowns, religious/occult glyphs, or humiliating rank labels.
- Do not mirror deterministic board geometry in RTL; only surrounding shell, text, action order, result hierarchy, and comparison adapt to locale.
- Do not make drag precision the only path. Sequential tap, pointer click, and keyboard remain first-class with the same score ceiling.
- Do not rely on color, sound, or motion alone. States require silhouette, outline, pattern, line state, and accessible-announcement support.
- Do not reveal the route in results, share imagery, link previews, comparison, or challenge entry.
- Do not include betting, monetary chance reward, public humiliation, public leaderboard, unsafe imitation, public UGC, chat, forced sharing, contact harvesting, or live multiplayer in the first slice.
- Keep audio unapproved and play complete in silence unless a later scoped issue validates a specific addition.
- Require pause, interruption recovery, lifecycle, replay reset, strict challenge-link validation, low-end behavior, reduced-motion equivalence, privacy-safe measurement, and repeated-session teardown.

## Diversity rule

A new game must differ materially from active games in at least two of:

- input;
- movement;
- failure;
- scoring;
- tension;
- social interaction;
- visual direction;
- 2D/3D presentation.

A visual reskin alone is not a new game.
