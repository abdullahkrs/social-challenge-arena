## Trace Relay concept constraints

- Keep the session short, understandable from a brief visual demonstration, and playable without registration.
- Do not imitate an operating-system unlock grid, Simon-style trade dress, known characters, existing level layouts, or recognizable branded presentation.
- Do not mirror deterministic board geometry in RTL; only the surrounding shell and directional UI adapt to locale.
- Do not make drag precision the only path. Sequential tap and keyboard must remain first-class.
- Do not reveal the route in shared result imagery or link previews.
- Do not include betting, monetary chance reward, public humiliation, public leaderboard, unsafe imitation, public UGC, chat, or live multiplayer in the first slice.
- Keep audio optional and silent-safe until Islamic Content Review records an approved approach.
- Require complete lifecycle, replay reset, strict challenge-link validation, low-end behavior, reduced-motion equivalence, and privacy-safe measurement.

## Diversity rule

A new game must differ materially from active games in at least two of:

- input;
- movement;
- failure;
- scoring;
- tension;
- social interaction;
- visual direction;
- 2D/3D presentation.

A visual reskin alone is not a new game.
