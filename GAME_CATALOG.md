## Trace Relay selected creative contract

- Selected treatment: **Ribbon Relay**.
- Core fantasy: receive an abstract route handoff, remember it, reproduce it, and pass the same hidden challenge to a friend.
- Round one: five anchors, four route selections.
- Round two: seven anchors, five route selections.
- Round three: nine anchors, six route selections, with at most one non-consecutive anchor revisit.
- Wrong input does not erase completed progress or restart the run.
- Optional show-next assistance reveals only the next required anchor and removes first-try mastery credit for that position.
- Finish-now creates a friendly partial result.
- The share-worthy result transforms the completed route into a generic three-band relay mark without field geometry, route segments, anchor order, seed, solution, or active-play screenshot.
- Rematch creates a fresh deterministic challenge; share-again is voluntary and never blocks continuation.

## Trace Relay concept constraints

- Keep the typical first session within roughly 30–45 seconds, understandable from a brief skippable visual demonstration, and playable without registration.
- Do not imitate an operating-system unlock grid, Simon-style trade dress, known memory toy, known character, existing level layout, branded palette, copied sound, copied wording, or recognizable branded presentation.
- Never use a standard 3×3 dot grid, security/unlock metaphor, four colored pads, cumulative one-more-step sound loop, hearts/lives, fail buzzers, skulls, flames, crowns, or humiliating rank labels.
- Do not mirror deterministic board geometry in RTL; only the surrounding shell, text, action order, result hierarchy, and comparison presentation adapt to locale.
- Do not make drag precision the only path. Sequential tap, pointer click, and keyboard must remain first-class and must retain the same score ceiling.
- Do not rely on color, sound, or motion alone. Anchors and route states require silhouette, outline, pattern, line-state, and accessible-announcement support.
- Do not reveal the route in results, share imagery, link previews, comparison, or challenge entry.
- Do not include betting, monetary chance reward, public humiliation, public leaderboard, unsafe imitation, public UGC, chat, forced sharing, contact harvesting, or live multiplayer in the first slice.
- Keep audio optional and silent-complete until Islamic Content Review records an approved approach.
- Require complete pause, interruption recovery, lifecycle, replay reset, strict challenge-link validation, low-end behavior, reduced-motion equivalence, privacy-safe measurement, and repeated-session teardown.
- Keep the decision in Review and implementation blocked until Islamic Content Review, Visual Direction, Motion Design, and focused architecture review complete.

## Specialist handoff

- Islamic Content Review: abstract ribbon, anchor glyph family, three-band result mark, celebration fragments, private social comparison tone, rewards, and every audio choice.
- Visual Direction: anchor and field system, route/ribbon treatment, full state matrix, contrast, HUD, result/comparison/share presentation, low-end fallback, asset provenance, and originality comparison.
- Motion Design: reveal cadence, concealment/input boundary, input feedback, error/assistance, transitions, relay-mark transformation, reduced-motion equivalent, effect budget, pause/replay/teardown.
- Architecture: rendering choice, deterministic seed, strict links, score formula and trust model, pause/timing rules, input fairness, accessibility naming, localization, lifecycle, privacy-safe metrics, and future 3D isolation.

## Diversity rule

A new game must differ materially from active games in at least two of:

- input;
- movement;
- failure;
- scoring;
- tension;
- social interaction;
- visual direction;
- 2D/3D presentation.

A visual reskin alone is not a new game.
