## Trace Relay selected creative contract

- Selected treatment: **Ribbon Relay**.
- Core fantasy: receive an abstract route handoff, remember it, reproduce it, and pass the same hidden challenge to a friend.
- Round one: five anchors, four route selections.
- Round two: seven anchors, five route selections.
- Round three: nine anchors, six route selections, with at most one non-consecutive anchor revisit.
- Wrong input does not erase completed progress or restart the run.
- Optional show-next assistance reveals only the next required anchor and removes first-try mastery for that position.
- Finish-now creates a friendly partial result.
- The share-worthy result transforms completed rounds into a generic three-band relay mark without field geometry, route segments, anchor order, seed, solution, or active-play screenshot.
- Rematch creates a fresh deterministic challenge; share-again is voluntary and never blocks continuation.

## Trace Relay concept constraints

- Keep the typical first session around 30–45 seconds, understandable from a brief skippable demonstration, and playable without registration.
- Do not imitate an operating-system unlock grid, Simon-style trade dress, known memory toy, branded palette, copied sound, copied wording, character, level, or recognizable presentation.
- Never use a standard 3×3 dot grid, security/unlock metaphor, four colored pads, cumulative sound loop, hearts/lives, fail buzzers, skulls, flames, crowns, or humiliating rank labels.
- Do not mirror deterministic board geometry in RTL; only surrounding shell, text, action order, result hierarchy, and comparison adapt to locale.
- Do not make drag precision the only path. Sequential tap, pointer click, and keyboard remain first-class with the same score ceiling.
- Do not rely on color, sound, or motion alone. States require silhouette, outline, pattern, line state, and accessible-announcement support.
- Do not reveal the route in results, share imagery, link previews, comparison, or challenge entry.
- Do not include betting, monetary chance reward, public humiliation, public leaderboard, unsafe imitation, public UGC, chat, forced sharing, contact harvesting, or live multiplayer in the first slice.
- Keep audio optional and play complete in silence.
- Require pause, interruption recovery, lifecycle, replay reset, strict challenge-link validation, low-end behavior, reduced-motion equivalence, privacy-safe measurement, and repeated-session teardown.

## Islamic review sequence

### Gate A — concept envelope

Immediate next review covers:

- abstract digital relay theme;
- no characters or clothing;
- neutral non-devotional environment;
- abstract symbol direction and forbidden symbol categories;
- score/progress/replay rewards only;
- private asynchronous comparison without humiliation or manipulation;
- deterministic generation without wager or monetary chance mechanics;
- silent-complete boundary with no audio approved yet.

A Gate A PASS authorizes Visual Direction and Motion Design to create specifications inside that envelope only.

### Gate B — final user-facing packet

After Visual Direction and Motion Design, mandatory review covers:

- exact anchor/glyph sheet and states;
- exact three-band relay mark and share presentation;
- celebration fragments, particles, and result transformation;
- final motion specification;
- every proposed audio element or explicit silent release;
- final challenge, result, assistance, comparison, and share wording;
- culturally or symbolically uncertain references.

The game remains in **Review** and architecture/implementation remain blocked until Gate B records PASS.

## Specialist handoff

- Islamic Content Review Gate A: concept envelope described above.
- Visual Direction after Gate A PASS: anchor and field system, route treatment, state matrix, contrast, HUD, result/comparison/share presentation, low-end fallback, asset provenance, originality comparison, and exact Gate B visual packet.
- Motion Design after Gate A PASS: reveal cadence, concealment/input boundary, input/error/assistance feedback, transitions, result transformation, reduced-motion equivalent, effect budget, pause/replay/teardown, and exact Gate B motion/audio packet.
- Islamic Content Review Gate B: exact final user-facing packet; PASS required before architecture.
- Architecture after Gate B PASS: rendering choice, deterministic seed, strict links, score formula and trust model, pause/timing rules, input fairness, accessibility naming, localization, lifecycle, privacy-safe metrics, and future 3D isolation.

## Diversity rule

A new game must differ materially from active games in at least two of:

- input;
- movement;
- failure;
- scoring;
- tension;
- social interaction;
- visual direction;
- 2D/3D presentation.

A visual reskin alone is not a new game.
