# Experiments

## Core Product Hypothesis

If a challenge can be understood in 5–10 seconds and shared as one link with a personal competitive prompt, then friends are more likely to click, play, compare, and share again.

## Viral Loop Experiments

### Experiment 1: Beat-my-score prompt

Prompt: “I got {score}. Can you beat me?”

Goal: Test whether direct competition increases friend clicks.

### Experiment 2: Identity challenge prompt

Prompt: “How well do you know me?”

Goal: Test whether personal curiosity beats generic score competition.

### Experiment 3: Funny result card

Prompt: Result includes a playful title, not just a number.

Goal: Test whether funny/flexible result cards increase sharing.

### Experiment 4: Creator version

Prompt: “My followers try to beat my score.”

Goal: Test whether creators can use one challenge link repeatedly.

### Experiment 5: WhatsApp-first copy

Prompt: Very short text optimized for forwarding to friends and groups.

Goal: Test whether WhatsApp sharing outperforms public posting.

## Initial Template Ideas

- 10-second reaction/choice quiz.
- Who knows me best?
- Guess my answer.
- Two-option voting challenge.
- Beat my score mini quiz.
- Daily “one question” challenge.

## Experiment Priority

Start with the smallest score/result loop first. Do not add more templates until the first loop works end-to-end.
